I am Emmanuel A. Atindama, a statistician and data scientist working at the intersection of mathematical modeling, experimental design, and data-driven decision making.

I currently support CMC and biopharmaceutical manufacturing workflows in New Brunswick, NJ as a statistician, with emphasis on stability analysis, comparability decisions, and statistical communication for filing and investigations.

My PhD research at Clarkson University focused on computational methods for restoring crystallographic data from EBSD microscopy, combining partial differential equation models with machine learning. This work led to the open-source tool [PyEBSD-SEM](https://github.com/emmanuelatindama/PyEBSD-SEM).

Before my current role, I served as a visiting assistant professor in data science at the University of Toledo and held data science and statistics roles at Clarkson University and Regeneron Pharmaceuticals.

My applied interests include equivalence testing, risk-aware manufacturing decisions, sampling plans, and interpretable analytics for scientific and operational teams.

I enjoy translating technical complexity into clear decisions through rigorous statistics, reproducible code, and strong communication.
