<p class="post-lead">I am Emmanuel A. Atindama, a statistician and data scientist working at the intersection of mathematical modeling, experimental design, and data-driven decision making.</p>

<section class="story-panel">
	<p>I currently support CMC and biopharmaceutical manufacturing workflows in New Brunswick, NJ as a statistician, with emphasis on stability analysis, comparability decisions, and statistical communication for filing and investigations.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-panels">
	<article class="story-panel">
		<h3>Research Foundation</h3>
		<p>My PhD research at Clarkson University focused on computational methods for restoring crystallographic data from EBSD microscopy, combining partial differential equation models with machine learning.</p>
		<p>This work led to the open-source tool <a href="https://github.com/emmanuelatindama/PyEBSD-SEM">PyEBSD-SEM</a>.</p>
	</article>
	<article class="story-panel">
		<h3>Professional Arc</h3>
		<p>Before my current role, I served as a visiting assistant professor in data science at the University of Toledo and held data science and statistics roles at Clarkson University and Regeneron Pharmaceuticals.</p>
	</article>
</section>
