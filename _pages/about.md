I am Emmanuel A. Atindama, a statistician and quantitative researcher focused on regulated biopharmaceutical development and manufacturing.

My work supports CMC and biopharmaceutical manufacturing in New Brunswick, NJ, where I design and implement statistical approaches for filings, investigations, tech transfer, and stability analysis. I also lead AI development for statistical analysis automation to support regulatory filings.

I earned my PhD in Applied Mathematics (2024) from [Clarkson University](https://www.clarkson.edu/), with research in computer vision and image reconstruction. This work led to [PyEBSD-SEM](https://github.com/emmanuelatindama/PyEBSD-SEM), an open-source package for EBSD data restoration.

## Current Focus

- CMC statistics and risk-aware manufacturing decisions
- Equivalence testing and acceptance-criteria calibration
- Stability modeling with reproducible implementation in R, Python, SAS, and Shiny
- Applied AI for reliable, auditable statistical workflows

## Professional Background

Recent roles include Statistician (CMC and Biopharmaceutical Manufacturing) at Bristol Myers Squibb and SOKOL GxP, Visiting Assistant Professor at the University of Toledo, and earlier statistics/data-science roles at Clarkson University and Regeneron.

For full details, see my [CV](/cv/) and [blog](/blog/).

I am open to collaborations where methodological rigor and practical outcomes both matter.
